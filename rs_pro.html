Long-Term Land Cover Changes around Pasoh Forest Reserve, Peninsular Malaysia 1998 - 2010

Intro to Remote Sensing -2022 Fall
Final Project
Grace Du,Yu-Yun Ruan

Introduction

This study investigates the impact of land use changes around Malaysia's Pasoh Forest Reserve on wild boar density and ecological shifts. Using Landsat 5 TM images from 1986 to 2012, the research focuses on contrasting land cover changes before and after a significant clearcutting period between 2001 and 2002. The study aims to analyze how these changes in land cover and agriculture area ratios affect the reserve's ecosystem.

The research employed TerrSet for land cover analysis. It imported data from Earth Explorer , created a 10km buffer around the reserve, and performed cluster analysis, leading to categorization into Forest, Crops, Residential/Commercial, soil, clouds, and Shadows. Supervised classification using maximum likelihood yielded the most accurate results for image change detection.

Result

Conclusion

The land cover changes in the Pasoh Forest Reserve between 1998, 2004, and 2010 revealed significant alterations. Soil and agriculture exhibited contrasting trends, with soil gaining substantial area in 1998-2004 while agriculture faced a decline. The reverse occurred in 2004-2010. Forest saw consistent losses throughout the period, highlighting significant alterations due to clearcutting and subsequent replanting. Data limitations, including the accuracy of the protected area boundary and cloud-covered 2004 data, pose challenges for future research, necessitating more precise data acquisition and analysis.
