Spatiotemporal Hotspot Analysis for Crime Record in Atlanta City, Georgia, before and after Covid Shutdown

Advanced Vector GIS -2022 Fall
Final Project
Weixuan Du,Yu-Yun Ruan

Introduction

After the COVID shutdown policy was implemented in the United States in March 2020, the overall crime rate and categories changed. This study examines variations in crime rates and types of crimes in Atlanta, Georgia, using criminal records from the same period before and after the shutdown. This study also used ArcGIS Pro conducts hotspot analysis to investigate how crime hotspots' distribution changed before and after the shutdown. The results show that the change in crime rate and type in the Atlanta area was similar to the overall crime rate in the United States. The hotspot research discovered no discernible difference in the distribution of crime hotspots.  Furthermore, this project compares the relationship between violent crime rate and Social Vulnerability Index (SVI), and the findings indicate no substantial relationship in this area.

Conclusion

The study analyzed crime rates and types in Atlanta before and after the COVID-19 shutdown. The crime rate was lowest one month after the shutdown, with larceny falling the most and vehicle theft increasing significantly. Space-time cube views revealed that crime frequency decreased before the shutdown but increased afterward, particularly in the east-middle area. Hotspot analysis showed concentrated hotspots before COVID-19 and more dispersed hotspots afterward. Violent crime analysis showed no significant geographic association with the Social Vulnerability Index (SVI). The crime rate decreased after the shutdown, with changing crime types and hotspot distribution. SVI showed little correlation with violent crime in Atlanta.
