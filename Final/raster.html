Advanced Raster GIS -2023 Fall

Climate change has wide-ranging and ongoing impacts, driven by human activities. These impacts affect vegetation health directly through management decisions and indirectly through temperature fluctuations. Vegetation productivity, an essential measure of ecological well-being, is influenced by various factors, including drought, climate change, urbanization, and management practices. Air quality is another significant concern, with anthropogenic changes in air quality contributing to climate change through feedback loops. Surprisingly, air quality is projected to worsen in some regions, contrary to expectations. Aerosols, both natural and anthropogenic, add complexity by either cooling or warming the atmosphere. Understanding the relationship between aerosols and vegetative properties, such as productivity and evapotranspiration, is crucial.

This study focuses on Massachusetts, a region experiencing environmental changes, to investigate the intricate interplay between air quality and vegetation. By analyzing trends and variations in vegetation indices and aerosol levels, the research aims to shed light on their mutual impact and contribute valuable insights to climate research efforts.
